Who Gets to Go Home?

Charlotte-Mecklenburg Hospital Readmissions: A Clinical & Regional Equity Audit

When my grandmother survived COVID-19 in 2020, her recovery exposed a chaotic, under-resourced healthcare loop. Wanting to audit these regional care disparities across Charlotte-Mecklenburg, I hit a massive roadblock: federal CMS registries intentionally suppressed readmission data from the first half of 2020 to shield hospital margins from pandemic penalties.

Scope of Project: Rather than abandon the audit due to this data gap and HIPAA privacy bounds, I engineered a highly realistic synthetic engine simulating 6,845 unique patient journeys. By anchoring this simulated data directly to real-world benchmarks from the "FY 2026 CMS Registry" and regional "DHHS datasets". This project exposes hidden post-discharge inequities actively impacting elderly, Medicaid, and minority cohorts.
